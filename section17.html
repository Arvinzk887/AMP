Chapter 10: Continuing Your Options Trading Education

Introduction

As the old adage goes, “The only constant in life is change.” This couldn’t be more true when it comes to trading. The markets are constantly evolving. New challenges arise, strategies come in and out of favor, and what worked yesterday may not work tomorrow.

In this dynamic landscape, continuous learning is the key to long-term success as an options trader. It allows you to adapt to new challenges, refine your strategies, and maintain your competitive edge.

Throughout my own three-decade journey as an options trader, I’ve witnessed firsthand the importance of ongoing education. From the early days of online trading to the explosion of online resources, I’ve had to continually update my knowledge and skills to stay competitive. Along the way, I’ve also encountered my fair share of “bad actors” – educators and gurus who overpromise, underdeliver, and often leave their students worse off than when they started.

These experiences have inspired me to write this chapter and share with you the lessons I’ve learned about continuing your options trading education effectively and responsibly. Central to this effort is a “15-step checklist” I’ve developed for evaluating trading educators and courses.

How to Approach Continued Education with a Critical Lens

Born out of my own trials and errors and my work reviewing numerous trading programs on my YouTube channel, this checklist is designed to help you navigate the often confusing landscape of trading education and make informed decisions about who to learn from.

As you seek out new learning opportunities, these questions can guide you to find the right educator to support your ongoing growth as a trader.

Let’s dive into each question and explore why it matters.

The 15-Step Trading Course Audit & Checklist
Transparency and Credibility
Question: Do you share your portfolio P&L?Yes or No
A transparent educator should be willing to share their real portfolio performance, including profits and losses. Don’t trust self-reported numbers. Ensure they share their real net liquidation value and snapshots/video recording of their trade history from a reputable broker.
Question: Do you share all the trades made in your account as you trade them with real money?Yes or No
Full transparency in real-time trade reporting is a sign of an educator’s confidence and integrity. While not always possible due to compliance or other reasons, real- time trade sharing can provide valuable learning opportunities.
Question: What kind of return should I expect if I follow?Yes or No
Be wary of educators who promise unrealistic returns or guarantee profits. A responsible educator should provide a range of reasonable expectations based on historical data and real-world performance.
Strategy Evaluation
Question: What is the edge? Is the edge explained?Yes or No
Every successful trading strategy must have an edge – a unique advantage that sets it apart from other approaches. Ask the educator to clearly define their edge and explain how it helps generate consistent profits.
Question: Do you recommend buying option premium or placing low-probability trades?Yes or No
Buying option premium or placing low-probability trades can be risky, especially for beginners. Be cautious of educators who heavily promote these approaches without a strong emphasis on risk management.
Question: Is the strategy directional?Yes or No
As we’ve discussed throughout this book, non-directional trading strategies can be more adaptable and effective in various market conditions. Consider whether the educator’s system relies heavily on predicting market direction.
System Evaluation
Question: Have you backtested or forward- tested the system in all market conditions? Including grind-down and crash scenarios?Yes or No
Backtesting and forward-testing are essential for validating the performance of a trading strategy. Ask the educator for detailed results of their testing process, including the methodology and time periods used.
Question: Does the educator trade with objective rules?Yes or No
A well-defined, rule-based trading system is crucial for consistency and reducing emotional decision-making. Look for educators who emphasize objective rules and provide clear guidelines for trade entry, management, and exit.
Question: Does the trade plan take into account commissions and slippage?Yes or No
Realistic trading plans should factor in the costs of commissions and slippage, as these can significantly impact overall profitability. Be cautious of educators who ignore or downplay these costs.
Risk Management
Question: In backtests, were there drawdowns? [Answer]
Every trading system will experience drawdowns – periods of losses or declines in account value. Ask the educator about the maximum drawdowns encountered during backtesting and how they plan to manage and mitigate these risks.
Question: What is the highest margin requirement allowed in the trade plan?[Answer]
Understanding the margin requirements of a trading strategy is essential for risk management and capital allocation. Ask the educator about the maximum margin requirements and ensure they align with your risk tolerance and account size.
Likelihood Of Success
Question: What is the likelihood of success for students following your approach? Are there already students with results or is it only you?Yes or No

An unfortunate industry secret is that many educators will have 1000s of customers yet they will only tout the success of 10 or so students. Those are great odds. Always request real case studies and success stories from past students to gauge the realistic chances of success with the educator’s program.

This underscore the importance of evaluating the educator’s track record in producing successful students. By requesting real case studies and success stories, you can gain valuable insights into the educator’s effectiveness and the likelihood of achieving your desired outcome.
Additional Considerations
Question: Is the trading system tax-advantaged?Yes or No
Certain trading strategies, such as those focused on index options like SPX, may offer tax advantages. Consider whether the educator’s system takes advantage of these benefits, which can help optimize your after-tax returns.
Question: Do I need to be in front of my computer most or all of the day to trade the system?Yes or No
A trading system that requires constant monitoring or frequent intraday actions may not be suitable for everyone. Consider your lifestyle and time constraints when evaluating an educator’s approach.
Question: Is the course proprietary?Yes or No
A proprietary course is one that teaches original, unique strategies developed by the educator. While not essential, a proprietary course may offer valuable insights and techniques not found elsewhere.

Now that you understand the significance of each question, let’s explore how to use them effectively when evaluating potential educators or courses.

  • Research the educator’s background and credentials. Look for verifiable track records, professional certifications, and industry experience.
  • Review the educator’s course materials, including any available syllabus, sample content, or testimonials from past students.
  • Schedule a consultation or attend a free webinar to get a feel for the educator’s teaching style and approach.
  • Ask the 15 questions during your consultation or via email, and carefully assess the educator’s responses.
  • Compare the educator’s answers to your own trading goals, risk tolerance, and learning preferences.
  • Trust your instincts – if something feels “off” or too good to be true, it probably is.
  • Remember “proof is in the pudding - If a trading educator gives excuses for not sharing their real P&L and verifiable track record of results (such as it being an income claim), then walk away. Showcasing verifiable past performance and brokerage statements does not constitute an “income claim” so long as it is substantiated and properly disclosed. Many fake gurus use the “income claim” excuse as a reason to not show their lackluster results.
  • Remember, the goal is to find an educator who aligns with your values, provides transparent and verifiable results, and offers a comprehensive, well-tested trading approach. Don’t be afraid to ask tough questions and walk away if an educator fails to meet your standards.

Conclusion

As you continue your options trading education, remember that the key to long-term success lies in continuous learning, adaptability, and a commitment to your own growth. Using the 15 questions provided in this chapter as a buyer’s guide, you’ll be well-equipped to navigate the often-confusing landscape of trading education and find the educators and resources that align with your goals.
