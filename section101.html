NOTE ABOUT TRADING PREREQUISITES:

Before we dive into the meat of this book, it is worth noting that the approach you will learn in this book is, in its most basic form, centered around options trading.

The reason I want to bring this up now is because trading is not for everyone. Nor should it be.

Most individuals lack the desire and dedication to outperform the market. They’d be better suited to buy and hold or enlist a financial advisor.

This book is not for the majority.

I believe specific prerequisites are necessary for you to get the most out of the strategies in this book.

Prerequisite number 1: Solid Foundation In Options Trading

Again, this book aims to show you how it’s possible to make money in all market environments using truly non-directional options strategies. When properly understood, I believe that options are one of the best passive income vehicles available.

However, the study of options involves a learning curve. While I’ll cover options trading in depth throughout this book, it’s helpful to have a basic understanding of how options work before diving in.

If the terms “CAGR”, “put”, “calls,” “Deltas,” or “butterfly” mean nothing to you, I have a helpful companion video training program to get you up to speed.

There is a video course called “Full Throttle Options Mastery,” and it’s perfect for you if you are new to options trading or need a perfect refresher. In this course, I break down every important option trading concept so you can build a solid foundation for a lifetime of successful options trading (and so you can get the most out of this book).

Prerequisite number 2: Desire To Manage Your Own Money

This book is for you if you are what I call a “freedom investor.” Freedom investors are individuals who want to control their own money and portfolios. They believe that if they don’t manage their money, no one will. They seek market outperformance and are willing to take their investments into their own hands.

If you do not desire to take your investments into your own hands, then this might not be for you.

Prerequisite Number 3: Realistic Expectations

There is no free lunch in trading. If you want to “get rich quick” or “double your account in months,” this book is NOT for you. I believe that aiming for such high returns can lead to overleveraging and a higher chance of blowing up your trading account. My strategies prioritize risk-adjusted returns, so if you’re solely focused on unrealistic gains, this might not be for you.

If you have the desire to manage your own money, realistic expectations, and a solid foundation in options trading, then you’re ready to dive in and start your journey to stock market success.
