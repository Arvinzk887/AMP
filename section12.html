Chapter 7 point 4: Simplified Trade Entry & Execution Principles

In the previous chapters, we’ve explored the essential components of the All-Market Profits Trading Framework, including the proactively hedged broken wing butterfly structure, the advantages of using SPAN or Portfolio Margin, and the importance of proper position sizing. With these foundational elements in place, we now turn our attention to the critical aspect of trade execution.

Executing trades effectively is a crucial skill for any options trader, as it directly impacts the overall performance and profitability of a trading strategy. In this chapter, we’ll dive into the concept of “campaign-style” trading, also known as laddering trades. Additionally, we’ll discuss the importance of using Single Ticket Orders when executing your trades, as this can help maintain Delta neutrality and reduce slippage.

The Power of Campaign-Style Trading

One of the key risk management components of an All-Market Profits type trading strategy is the concept of “campaign- style” trading. Also referred to as laddering or layering trades, campaign-style trading offers the ability to better diversify an options portfolio.

What Is Campaign-Style Trading (Laddering Trades)?

Campaign-style trading involves placing trades over time or at different periods based on a specific variable. Instead of putting all your eggs in one basket and executing a single large trade with however many contracts your position sizing guidelines permit, you spread out your trades across different layers based on different timeframes.

This approach creates a campaign, an ongoing system of layering in multiple trades to the portfolio that diversifies the portfolio across time and volatility. This campaign method helps diversify the risk aspects of any one individual trade amongst the entire group and allows the “older” more established trades to help hedge the “younger” more exposed trades. It’s similar to the concept of “dollar cost averaging” we’re spreading out “risk” in an organized and orderly manner.

In order to best illustrate how to execute campaign-style trading, it’s first important to define key terms.

Key Terms in Campaign-Style Trading:

  1. Portfolio: The collection or container of all your trades, layers, or positions.
  2. Trade, Position, or Layer: These terms are used interchangeably and refer to an individual position within the portfolio. The term “layer” is used most often when referencing an individual position.
  3. Tranche: A pre-configured grouping or basket of several different options contracts. One tranche is equivalent to a specific number of contracts, such as 5 contracts in the A.M.P. Structure.

Building Blocks of an All-Markets “Campaign-Style” Options Portfolio

A fully allocated portfolio can consist of any number of different layers you choose based on your preference, capital, etc. I typically break my full portfolio into four different layers. Each layer represents an individual trade or position and is one-fourth of the maximum allocated capital for four layers.

The campaign-style trading approach involves staging the entry of trades or layers over intervals of weeks to several weeks. This means you start with an empty portfolio and gradually add layers over time. Then, once the portfolio is fully allocated, you simply replace older layers with new layers.

For example, you might initiate your first trade or layer, and a week or more goes by before you add another layer. You continue this process, potentially holding onto previous layers while waiting for them to hit profit targets or other exit rules. A fully allocated portfolio would have all four layers active at once, with no additional layers added beyond that point.

The Benefits of Campaign-Style Trading (Laddering Trades):

  1. Diversification Across Time: Laddering trades across different time horizons offers several key benefits. First, it can help smooth out your portfolio’s performance by offsetting potential short-term losses with gains from longer-term trades. Second, it reduces the impact of market timing, as the success of your portfolio is less dependent on the precise entry point of any single trade. Finally, having traded with varying expiration dates allows you to adapt quickly to changing market conditions.
  2. Diversification Across Volatility: By placing trades at different volatility levels, you can avoid having all your eggs in one basket based on the volatility when the trade was initiated. Because volatility tends to revert to the mean, this can provide increased risk management. Additionally, mature layers tend to have different profit and risk profiles so they can help diversify the portfolio in certain markets.
  3. Negative Charm and Mature Structures: Campaignstyle trading also takes advantage of the concept of negative Charm, which is particularly beneficial in grind-down markets. As the market grinds down, the profit hump (or point of increasing profitability) of mature structures grows larger due to negative Charm, protecting the less mature structures in the portfolio. The larger the profit hump and the more negative the Delta, the more protection the mature structure offers to the less mature structures in the portfolio.

TRADE EXECUTION PRINCIPLES:

Beyond laddering my trades, I like to follow certain “trade execution” principles when executing my All-Market Profits- type strategies.

Because the All-Market Profits 1-2-2 option structure broken wing butterfly has multiple legs to the structure, I always aim to place my trades using Single Ticket Orders.

What Is a Single Ticket Order?

A Single Ticket Order involves putting on the entire position with one order rather than breaking the trade into multiple orders—commonly referred to as “legging in” and “legging out.” Because the broken wing butterfly has multiple legs in the structure, placing the trade on a single ticket simply means ensuring all legs of the trade (the upper long, middle short, and lower long) are all executed with one single limit order within the broker platform.

It is possible to manage an options portfolio without Single Ticket Orders, but I prefer to avoid “legging in” and “legging out” of my positions for the following reasons:

Maintaining Delta Neutrality & Reducing Slippage

Recall one of my goals is to enter all of my positions with Delta neutrality. If I were to execute my trade over several orders, directional risk could creep in as the underlying price can swing between the execution of each leg. If you don’t keep your Deltas flat (or neutral) during this process, the second leg order may need to be filled as soon as possible just in case the market moves against you. When you are rushed to execute an order, you usually get a bad fill, which means YOU lose money on slippage.

Good Til Canceled Profit Target

Single Ticket Orders have a serious advantage over “legging out” when you EXIT a position. With a multi-leg order position, you need to track the point where you hit your profit target. Only then do you take the trade-off. This moment of opportunity may only occur for a short period of time. If you miss it, it may take days or weeks or never happen again.

When executing trades using Single Ticket Orders, you can determine what price the position needs to fill in order to hit your profit target. You can place the order right after you enter the position. Just let the order sit with a Good Until Cancel type order. Even if the profit target is hit for just an instance, you don’t need to be present for the trade to execute. I have seen my orders get filled and then watched the market go right back to what would have been a non-profitable price. There are those times when moments of liquidity come into the market for a flash to fill your order.

Chapter Summary

In this chapter, we focused on the critical aspect of trade execution, introducing the concept of “campaign-style” trading, also known as laddering trades. Campaign-style trading involves spreading out trades across different layers based on specific timeframes, creating an ongoing system of diversifying the portfolio across time and volatility. Additionally, we emphasized the importance of using Single Ticket Orders when executing trades to maintain Delta neutrality and reduce slippage.

Key Chapter Takeaways

  1. Campaign-style trading (laddering trades) is a key risk management component of All-Market Profit-types strategies, involving placing trades over time to create a diversified options portfolio.
    1. A fully allocated portfolio consists of multiple layers, each representing an individual trade or position, with older layers being replaced by new ones as the portfolio evolves.
    2. Campaign-style trading offers benefits such as diversification across time and volatility, allowing traders to better navigate challenging market conditions and manage risk effectively.
  2. Single Ticket Orders are preferred when executing trades to maintain Delta neutrality and reduce slippage.
    1. Placing the entire position with one order, rather than breaking the trade into multiple orders, helps maintain Delta neutrality and reduces the risk of directional exposure.
    2. Single Ticket Orders also allow for more efficient trade management, particularly when exiting positions, by enabling traders to set “good until canceled” (GTC) profit targets.
