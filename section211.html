The Rising Bull Market

Definition: A Rising Bull Market can be defined as a sustained period in which the market continually rises and there is relatively low volatility. 

Example: For the entire year of 2017, there was a Rising Bull Market. The market (SPX or S&P 500 stock market) rose over 19 percent while the highest VIX reading was under 17 point 5. 

A.M.P. Strategy Performance: From December 24, 2018 to February 19, 2020, the market (S&P 500) rose by 44 point zero two percent while the A.M.P. rose by 14 point three nine percen . From March 24, 2020 to January 3, 2020, the market (S&P 500) rose by 114 point three eight percent while the A.M.P. rose by 72 point nine three percent. 

All-Market Profit strategies perform well in bull markets primarily due to their positive Theta exposure. As the market rises, the strategy benefits from time decay while maintaining a non-directional stance, allowing it to capture profits without relying on directional bets.

The Crash Market

Definition: A crash market is a sudden and significant drop in stock prices, typically defined as a decline of about 10% or more within a few days.

Example: A Crash Market occurred in August 2015 when the market dropped 13 point one three percent in four days and the VIX went from 15 to over 53, a 38-point spike. Another Crash Market occurred in early February 2018 when the market fell over 10 point 5 percent in seven days and the VIX went from 12 point 5 to over 50 in four days. More recently, The March 2020 COVID Crash saw The S&P 500 drop by approximately 34 percent in just over a month. Simultaneously, the VIX soared, jumping from around 15 to a peak of 82 point six nine. 

A.M.P. Strategy Performance: In the flash crash of August 2015, the S&P 500 spiraled nearly 11 percent while the A.M.P. gained 5 point four one percent in the same period. Then, in the crash of February 2018, the market dropped nearly 8 point 5 percent while this strategy gained 5 point three three percent. Finally, in the COVID crash, the market dropped nearly 34 percent while this strategy only drew down 2 point two seven percent. All-Market Profit strategies exhibit remarkable resilience in crash markets due to their proactive hedging and long volatility exposure. By incorporating strategic hedges and harnessing the power of Vomma, these strategies can potentially generate profits, even during sharp market declines.
