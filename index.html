Don’t be like me.

On this quest to trade for a living, I tried fundamental analysis and stock picking, but that didn’t meet my goals.

That dream was taken away from me one day: September 30th, 2008.

The fateful Monday was one of the early days of the “Great Recession.” President Bush was supposed to sign off on the TARP relief program. When he failed to do that, the market went into a tailspin. The VIX volatility index spiked to almost 70, and my account blew up! 

Not knowing what to do, I sat paralyzed in my home office chair, only getting up once to pick up a call on my landline phone. It was a gentleman from TD Ameritrade - my broker at the time.

Reality soon began to set in, and I realized this call was the last call any trader ever wanted to receive. I was getting margin called, and I needed to liquidate my positions. My stomach sank, thinking I had just lost years of growing my assets, my trading account, and my life savings.

Not only did I have to close all of my positions due to massive losses but the broker also told me that I owed $125,000 on top of what I had already lost!

As you can imagine, I was in bad shape and desperate: I had to borrow against my credit cards to pay off my broker. My stress spiked just as hard as the market crashed. It was so bad that the asthma I had as a child suddenly came back - decades after I last experienced it.

Here I was, 38 years old, with a wife and four children. I had started trading in the markets with the dream of providing for my family and retiring early. Instead, I was in debt, I had 15 no trading capital, and now I had to walk around, not only with my self-imposed shame but also with an inhaler in my pocket for my newfound asthma.

Fortunately, I was beaten down but not broken. ALL-MARKET PERFORMANCE

We will discuss this in further detail in Chapter 6 of this book, but what sets the All-Market Profits-type strategies apart is their ability to consistently outperform across various market environments.

At a high level, here is a quick breakdown of the common options selling market types and a snapshot of how an A.M.P.-style system performs. 

For option premium sellers, the market environment can be broken down into four categories: Sideways, Grind Downs, Rising Bulls, and Crash Market.

The Sideways Market

Definition: A Sideways Market is where prices fluctuate within a narrow range without forming any distinct trend upwards or downwards.

Example: A Sideways Market in the first six months of 2015 when the market stayed within a 4% range up or down and the VIX high was slightly above 23. 

A.M.P. Strategy Performance: From 1/2/2015 - 7/27/2015, the market (S&P 500) only rose by 0.46% while the A.M.P. increased by 10.09%. 

From 7/15/2016 - 11/16/2016, the market (S&P 500) only rose by 0.70% while this A.M.P. strategy increased by 5.88%. 

All-Market Profits strategies excel in sideways markets primarily due to their non-directional setup and positive Theta exposure.

The Grind-Down Market

 Definition: A Grind-Down Market is a sustained period where the market prices decline by a certain percentage (around 10%+) over a given timeframe.

Example: A Grind-Down Market occurred from October 1, 2018, through December 31, 2018, when the market was down 14.5% with a maximum VIX spike of approximately 25 points.

A.M.P. Strategy Performance: From 9/20/2018 - 12/24/2018, the market (S&P 500) drew down by -19.78% while the A.M.P. increased by 11.57%. From 1/03/2022 - 10/12/2022, the market (S&P 500) drew down by -25.43% while the A.M.P. increased by 18.69%.

All-Market Profit strategies demonstrate resilience in grinddown markets by harnessing the power of the higher-order Greek known as Charm.

The Rising Bull Market

Definition: A Rising Bull Market can be defined as a sustained period in which the market continually rises and there is relatively low volatility. 

Example: For the entire year of 2017, there was a Rising Bull Market. The market (SPX or S&P 500 stock market) rose over 19% while the highest VIX reading was under 17.5. 

A.M.P. Strategy Performance: From 12/24/2018 to 2/19/2020, the market (S&P 500) rose by 44.02% while the A.M.P. rose by 14.39%. From 3/24/2020 - 1/3/2022, the market (S&P 500) rose by 114.38% while the A.M.P. rose by 72.93%. 

All-Market Profit strategies perform well in bull markets primarily due to their positive Theta exposure. As the market rises, the strategy benefits from time decay while maintaining a non-directional stance, allowing it to capture profits without relying on directional bets.

The Crash Market

Definition: A crash market is a sudden and significant drop in stock prices, typically defined as a decline of about 10% or more within a few days.

Example: A Crash Market occurred in August 2015 when the market dropped 13.13% in four days and the VIX went from 15 to over 53, a 38-point spike. Another Crash Market occurred in early February 2018 when the market fell over 10.5% in seven days and the VIX went from 12.5 to over 50 in four days. More recently, The March 2020 COVID Crash saw The S&P 500 drop by approximately 34% in just over a month. Simultaneously, the VIX soared, jumping from around 15 to a peak of 82.69. 

A.M.P. Strategy Performance: In the flash crash of August 2015, the S&P500 spiraled nearly 11% while the A.M.P. gained 5.41% in the same period. Then, in the crash of February 2018, the market dropped nearly 8.5% while this strategy gained 5.33%. Finally, in the COVID crash, the market dropped nearly 34% while this strategy only drew down 2.27%. All-Market Profit strategies exhibit remarkable resilience in crash markets due to their proactive hedging and long volatility exposure. By incorporating strategic hedges and harnessing the power of Vomma, these strategies can potentially generate profits, even during sharp market declines.

Nathaniel is a self-employed trader from Connecticut who has been actively involved in the stock market since graduating college in 2019. His trading experience spans longterm investing, swing trading, day trading, and non-directional options strategies.

Despite his mathematical background and intermediate-level understanding of options trading, Nathaniel struggled to find a reliable, stress-free approach to building wealth in the markets.

After following my work for several years and successfully implementing his other trading strategies, Nathaniel decided to enroll in the All-Market Profits mentorship program. The experience was transformative.

In Nathaniel’s own words, “I swear I’m not exaggerating. It really feels like there’s a little bit of a light at the end of the tunnel in terms of being able to relax a little bit, work a little less, and be away from my computer a little bit more. For the first time in a long time, I’m really feeling like this strategy could really change my life. I’m going to trade this for decades.”

The key benefits Nathaniel found in the All-Market Profits trading include:

  • The extensive backtesting across various market conditions gave him unshakeable confidence in the strategy’s durability and long-term profitability. Unlike his previous trading systems that quickly lost their edge, the AMS methodology had proven resilient year after year.
  • The systematic, rules-based approach removed the stress and subjectivity of his active trading days. Nathaniel could now manage his portfolio with minimal time commitment without being glued to his screens.
  • The strategy’s defensive nature and lower drawdowns aligned perfectly with his risk-averse nature. As a longterm investor, Nathaniel appreciated how the AMS approach offered steadier returns than buy-and-hold with far milder drawdowns.

Nathaniel plans to allocate up to two-thirds of his net worth to the All-Market Profits trading strategies with the goal of potentially increasing that allocation up to 100% as his confidence in navigating margin expansion grows. He sees this not just as an investing strategy but as a vehicle to achieve his early retirement goals by generating reliable, stress-free passive income.
