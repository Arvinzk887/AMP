Chapter 9: Choosing the Best Broker for All-Market Profits

Now that you’ve learned the steps for effectively validating and refining your trading ideas through backtesting, it’s time to take the next crucial step in your trading journey: choosing the right broker.

Even if you have a broker that you are currently satisfied with, it is important to ensure that your broker fits with your trading needs. Your choice of broker can significantly impact your trading experience, from the tools and resources available to you to the commissions and fees you’ll pay.

In this chapter, we’ll guide you through the process of choosing the best broker for your individual trading requirements. We’ll discuss the key factors to consider, such as platform features, commissions and fees, margin requirements, and customer support. By the end of this chapter, I aim to equip you with the knowledge needed so you can select a broker that aligns with your trading goals and style.

Understanding Your Trading Requirements:

Before diving into the comparison of different brokerages, it’s essential to take a step back and assess your own trading requirements. As an options trader, you’ll need a brokerage that offers a robust platform with the necessary tools and features to support your trading style.

Consider the following factors as prerequisites when evaluating your trading needs:

  1. Options trading level: Trading levels, also known as approval levels, protect both the broker and the customer. Options brokers are regulated and have a duty to look out for the best interests of their customers, which gives them an obligation to ensure that their customers only take risks for which they have sufficient experience and funds.

    Most options brokers assign trading levels from 1 to 5 with 1 being the lowest and 5 being the highest. A trader with a low trading level will be fairly limited in the strategies they can use, while one with the highest will be able to make pretty much whatever trade they want. Ensure that the brokerage you choose supports the level of options trading you intend to pursue, whether it’s basic, intermediate, or advanced.

  2. Platform features: Look for a brokerage that provides a user-friendly platform with advanced charting, risk management tools, and real-time data.
  3. Commissions and fees: Compare the commission structures and fees across different brokerages to ensure you’re getting a competitive rate.
  4. Margin requirements: If you plan to use margin or trade more advanced strategies, ensure that the brokerage offers the appropriate margin requirements and account types (e.g., Portfolio Margin, SPAN Margin).

Zero-Commission Brokers: The Hidden Costs

I believe it’s important to quickly highlight why I didn’t include any zero-commission brokers that have been gaining popularity in recent years such as Robinhood and Webull have gained popularity among retail traders. Though these platforms offer commission-free trades it’s essential to understand the potential hidden costs of these brokers.

The old adage “if the service is free, you’re the product” holds true in the case of zero-commission brokers. These platforms often generate revenue by selling their users’ order flow to market makers and high-frequency trading firms. This practice, known as payment for order flow (PFOF), allows these firms to gain valuable insights into retail traders’ behavior and potentially exploit this information for their own benefit.

When a broker sells your order flow, it means that your trades may not be executed at the best available price, as the market makers and high-frequency traders can manipulate the bid-ask spread to their advantage. This can result in hidden costs for traders, as they may end up paying more for their trades than they would with a traditional, commission- based broker.

Moreover, the practice of selling order flow can create conflicts of interest, as the broker’s incentives may not align with their clients’ best interests. This lack of transparency and potential for suboptimal trade execution should be a red flag for traders seeking a reliable, trustworthy brokerage partner.

Let’s now run through FIVE important criteria to help guide your process of choosing a suitable broker for your trading needs:

Criteria Number 1: Availability and Account Requirements

When choosing a brokerage, it’s crucial to ensure that it’s available in your country and that you meet the account requirements. While most brokerages on our list are available in the United States, some, like Interactive Brokers and tastytrade, have a more extensive global presence. Additionally, some brokerages may have higher minimum account balance requirements for certain features, such as Portfolio Margin.

Criteria Number 2: Options Trading Platforms and Tools

A powerful and user-friendly trading platform is essential for options traders. Think or swim, Interactive Brokers, and tasty works stand out for their robust platforms, offering advanced charting, risk management tools, and customizable layouts. E TRADE and Fidelity also provide solid platforms, although they may not be as feature-rich as the top contenders.

Criteria Number 3: Commissions and Fees

While most brokerages now offer competitive commission rates, it’s still essential to compare the fees across different brokerages. Tradier, for example, offers a flat-fee pricing structure that may be attractive to high-volume traders. However, keep in mind that the lowest commissions may not always be the best choice if the brokerage lacks the features and tools you need.

Criteria Number 4:Margin Requirements and Advanced Features

For traders looking to employ more advanced strategies or trade with higher leverage, Portfolio Margin and SPAN Margin are crucial features to consider. Think or swim, Interactive Brokers, and tasty works offer both Portfolio Margin and SPAN Margin, making them suitable for advanced traders. E TRADE and Fidelity provide Portfolio Margin but lack SPAN Margin, while Tradier and TradeStation have more limited offerings in this area.

Criteria Number 5: Customer Support and Education

As you embark on your options trading journey, having access to reliable customer support and educational resources can be invaluable. Consider the level of support each brokerage offers, such as live chat, phone assistance, and email support. Additionally, look for brokerages that provide a wide range of educational materials, including webinars, tutorials, and in-depth guides, to help you continually improve your trading skills.

DYOR: Do Your Own Research

While this chapter has provided a detailed overview of the factors to consider when choosing a broker, it’s essential to conduct your own research to ensure you find the best fit for your specific needs. There are many valuable “broker comparison” sites online that offer detailed reviews and rankings of online brokers based on various criteria.

In addition to using comparison websites, be sure to visit the official websites of the brokers you’re considering. Take the time to explore their platforms, read through their pricing and fee structures, and review their educational resources and customer support offerings. Many brokers offer demo accounts, which allow you to test their platforms and tools without risking real money. Take advantage of these opportunities to get a firsthand feel for each broker’s offerings.

Finally, keep an eye out for sign-up bonuses or promotions offered by brokers. Some brokers may offer incentives such as free trades, cash bonuses, or educational resources to new account holders. While these bonuses shouldn’t be the sole deciding factor in your choice of broker, they can provide additional value and support as you begin your options trading journey.

Putting It All Together

Ultimately, the best brokerage for you will depend on your individual trading needs and preferences. By carefully evaluating your requirements and comparing the features and offerings of different brokerages, you can make an informed decision that sets you up for success in your options trading journey.

As you progress in your trading journey, you may find that your needs evolve, and you may need to reassess your brokerage choice. Don’t be afraid to make a change if you outgrow your current brokerage or find that it no longer meets your needs.

Key Takeaways

  1. Choosing the right broker is a crucial step in your options trading journey, as it can significantly impact your trading experience and success.
  2. Before selecting a broker, assess your trading requirements, including options trading level, platform features, commissions and fees, and margin requirements.
  3. Compare top brokerages across various criteria, such as availability, account requirements, trading platforms, tools, commissions, margin requirements, customer support, and educational resources.
  4. Be cautious of zero-commission brokers, as they may sell your order flow to market makers and high-frequency trading firms, potentially resulting in hidden costs and suboptimal trade execution.
  5. Look for brokers that offer advanced features like Portfolio Margin and SPAN Margin if you plan to employ more advanced strategies or trade with higher leverage.
  6. Consider the level of customer support and educational resources provided by each brokerage to ensure you have access to the assistance and guidance you need.
  7. Remember that your choice of broker is not set in stone; as your trading needs evolve, be prepared to reassess your brokerage relationship and make changes as necessary.
  8. Focus on finding a brokerage partner that prioritizes your interests, provides transparent pricing, and offers a robust, reliable trading platform to support your longterm success as an options trader.
