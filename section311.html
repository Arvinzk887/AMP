TRADER PITFALL #4: BELIEVING HIGH-WIN RATES & RETURNS = SUCCESS

Many traders (myself included) initially turn to selling options due to the high win rates and statistical edge the approach offers. High probability strategies, such as the traditional wheel options strategy, selling credit spreads, or anything recommended on tastytrade, might make you feel unstoppable, invincible, and overconfident, but there’s a trap.

High win rates might seem attractive, but they don’t necessarily equal success. Theta decay is not necessarily your savior if one or two bad trades can wipe out your account.

I call this the 95 percent Win-Rate Trap.

You may have even been here before. You’re trading along, winning trade after trade, collecting your weekly paychecks and making a killing at 30 percent to 60 percent plus a year, thinking, “I cracked the code.” Then, you continue to get more and more confident and continue to leverage up without truly understanding the risk. But, what happens?

It’s what typically happens to many of the “high-win rate” touting gurus. They proudly proclaim their 95 percent win rate but conveniently omit the impact of market crashes. Market crashes can and will wreak havoc on high-win rate portfolios, leading to significant drawdowns or unrecoverable losses.

Unfortunately, these gurus seldom address or even acknowledge this critical aspect. Again a high win rate is a vanity metric. This is why many traders liken selling premiums to picking up pennies in front of a steamroller because one bad trade can wipe out months or years of profit. They are often betting the empire for a pot of gold.

The biggest impediment to building stock market wealth.

Similarly to prioritizing high-win rates, many traders also tend to prioritize high returns over high-risk-adjusted returns. Outperforming isn’t just about achieving a higher percentage of returns, it’s also about considering the amount of risk you are taking. The real enemy of a trading system and profits is drawdowns.

Warren Buffett is famously quoted as saying: “The first rule of an investment is don’t lose [money]. And the second rule of an investment is don’t forget the first rule.”

No seasoned investor or trader will ever argue with that logic. However, it is worth including in this book because I don’t think most traders and investors fully understand why drawdowns are the biggest impediment to building stock market wealth.

As you probably know, a drawdown refers to the reduction in the value of your investment account from its peak. It occurs when your account value decreases, often due to market losses or unfavorable trading decisions. Your portfolio is either making all-time highs or recovering from drawdowns.

Understanding drawdowns is vital because they directly affect your portfolio’s performance and ability to recover from losses.

  • With a 30 Percent drawdown, you require a 43 Percent return to recover.
  • A 50 Percent drawdown means you need a 100 Percent return to getback to your starting point.

To put this in perspective, if a Hundred Thousad Dollar portfolio suffers a 50 Percent drawdown, it drops to Fifty Thousad Dollars. To return to hundred thousand dollars , a 100 Percent return is needed - meaning you would have to double your account just to get back to breakeven.

For this reason, it’s important to mitigate drawdowns as much as possible, as larger drawdowns require disproportionately larger returns to recover - it becomes an up hill battle to regain your portfolio’s initial value.

The True Cost of Drawdowns:

Managing a portfolio effectively means keeping drawdowns to a minimum not only for the financial implications but also for your emotional well-being. Severe drawdowns can lead to irrational decision-making, overtrading, sleepless nights, and heightened stress and anxiety. Heck! When I last blew up my account in 2008, this “drawdown” plagued me with 5 years of asthma.
