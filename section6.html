Chapter 5: The Problem with a Truly Non- Directional “All-Market Profits” Trading Strategy

In the previous chapters, we explored the numerous benefits of employing a truly non-directional trading strategy, culminating in the introduction of the proactively hedged broken wing butterfly as the cornerstone options structure for all-market profitability. Looking forward to Part 2 of this book, I will reveal the exact blueprint and methodology, including the constructions, position sizing, entry, and management guidelines. Packaged together, this forms the basis of a Truly Non-Directional Trading Strategy – or the All-Market Profits Framework.

While a truly non-directional trading strategy offers a robust foundation for navigating various market conditions, it is essential to acknowledge that no trading strategy is perfect, and there are inherent trade-offs to consider. I believe it’s important to address these limitations before introducing the full strategy to ensure proper expectations and provide a balanced perspective on what the A.M.P. can and cannot achieve.

It is not “get rich quick:”

First and foremost, it is essential to recognize that the A.M.P. is not a “get rich quick” scheme. If you are seeking to double your account in a matter of months or chase the thrill of highrisk, high-reward trades, then this strategy is not for you. The A.M.P. is designed for traders who prioritize steady, reliable returns over rapid account growth. It is a strategy for those who understand that slow and steady wins the race, and that preserving capital is just as important as growing it.

It does not outperform the market every year:

I believe a truly non-directional trading strategy offers a more reliable pathway to trading for a living and performing in all market types. However, performance in all market types does not mean OUTPERFORMANCE in EVERY market type.

Since the A.M.P. is a hedged options selling strategy that emphasizes protecting and growing capital across all market conditions, it does not perform “as well” in bull markets with low volatility. In some years, like 2017 and 2023, when there is extremely low volatility combined with a “melt-up” style bull run, it is difficult for hedge premium selling to keep up.

This is a deliberate trade-off – by prioritizing protection against significant drawdowns, the A.M.P. may sacrifice some potential upside during periods of sustained market growth. It is crucial to have realistic expectations and understand that the strategy’s focus on capital preservation may lead to underperformance on a “purely return basis” in some years.

It is not “set and forget:”

Another important consideration is that while the A.M.P. requires less frequent adjustments compared to many other trading strategies, it is not entirely hands-off. Traders should expect to make adjustments to their positions every 10 days or so, depending on market conditions.

This ongoing management is a necessary part of ensuring that it remains responsive to changing market dynamics. Traders who are looking for a completely passive investment approach that requires no regular attention may find that the A.M.P. does not meet their needs.

It is not the “end all be all:”

Once again, I am a non-directional trader. I don’t believe I can predict the future and I personally believe having a truly non-directional trading system offers a more reliable pathway to trading for a living and performing in all market types.

That said, traders with directional bias can outperform. That’s why it’s essential to consider the diversification of your overall investment and trading approaches. With that said, it is entirely possible to trade All-Market Profit-type strategies on top of a long bond or stock portfolio through a concept called “Profit Stacking,” which we will cover in the next section of this book.

Conclusion

The All-Market Profit-type strategies are a robust, non-directional trading methodology that prioritizes capital preservation and steady returns across a wide range of market conditions. However, like any strategy, it comes with its own set of limitations and trade-offs.

By understanding and embracing these characteristics – from its focus on gradual account growth to its need for regular management and its potential for underperformance in certain market types – traders can make informed decisions about whether the A.M.P. aligns with their individual goals and risk tolerance.

As we move forward into Part 2 of this book, we will dive deeper into the specifics of the All-Market Profits Trading Framework, exploring the key components that make this strategy so effective in navigating the complexities of options trading. From the proactively hedged broken wing butterfly structure to the advanced techniques of Profit Stacking, you will gain the knowledge and tools necessary to implement this powerful approach in your own trading.

So, let’s turn the page and embark on this exciting journey together as we unlock the secrets of the All-Market Profits framework and discover how it can help you achieve performance in all markets.
