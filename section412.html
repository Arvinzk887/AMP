The Solution?

Number 1 - Learn How to “Properly” Diversify (Covered in the Next Section Along with Part 2 of This Book)

Many attempts to minimize drawdowns, such as traditional diversification approaches, are often flawed. Each type of market presents challenges that need to be addressed to tame drawdowns.

Number 2 - Prioritize Risk-Adjusted Returns over Pure Returns When Determining a Strategy

If your goal is market outperformance, paying attention to your return on risk is critical. If a portfolio gains 20 percent but experiences a 60 percent drawdown, the returns are not as impressive. The emphasis on capital preservation and maintaining high-risk-adjusted returns are what separates the professional from the non-professional trader. If you can keep your drawdowns low and preserve your capital, you can continuously compound your returns.

HOW THE A.M.P. SOLVES THIS:

Relying on high-win rates and returns without properly assessing risk can lead to significant pain and emotional distress in trading. By confronting these potential drawdowns head-on and focusing on risk-adjusted returns and longterm sustainability, you can develop a more robust and reliable trading approach. The All-Market Profits framework, as discussed in the upcoming chapters, prioritizes these key factors to help you achieve consistent success while minimizing the pain of unexpected losses.

The only way to get low drawdowns is to weather all markets. The only way I’ve found to weather all markets is with non-directional options that are truly diversified (which we will cover in the next pitfall).
