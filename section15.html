Chapter 8: Backtest Before You Invest

Introduction

One of the most critical skills you can develop as an options trader is the ability to effectively backtest your trading ideas. Throughout my 30-year trading career, I’ve spent over 10,000 hours backtesting. It’s been a crucial part of my journey, allowing me to experiment, validate, refine, and improve my strategies.

In this chapter, we’ll dive into the concept of backtesting, explore its importance, and guide you through the essential steps I take when I backtest my trade idea. To begin, let’s define backtesting exactly.

What is backtesting, and why is it important?

Backtesting is a cornerstone technique in options trading, providing traders with a way to evaluate their strategies using historical data. At its core, backtesting is a form of scenario analysis where we “stress test” our plan for a stated objective and use comparative analysis against benchmarks. Extensively testing a trading strategy across various market conditions is critical for modeling potential future performance and assessing risk profiles.

I firmly believe that if you cannot backtest your approach, you cannot create a predictive model in which you can be confident in investing. With backtesting, you can test multiple variations of your strategy, experiment with different parameters, and learn from your mistakes without risking real capital.

Furthermore, backtesting is akin to “paper trading” because it provides a risk-free environment to practice and refine your trading strategy in historical and live trading environments using “paper money” accounts. This process of manual backtesting not only allows you to test and optimize your trading plan but also helps you develop the mental fortitude and discipline needed to execute your strategy consistently, even in challenging market conditions.

By manually backtesting your trades, you gain valuable experience in managing your emotions and staying calm under pressure, particularly when market movements don’t align with your expectations or when facing a market crash. This practice helps you build the confidence and resilience needed to stick to your plan and make rational decisions, even in the face of adversity.

Moreover, the repetition and practice involved in manual backtesting can help ingrain your trading plan into your subconscious mind, making it easier to execute your strategy consistently and without hesitation in live trading situations. As the saying goes, “Perfect practice makes perfect,” and by dedicating time and effort to thorough backtesting, you’ll be better prepared to navigate the challenges and opportunities of real-world trading with poise and precision.

While past performance doesn’t guarantee future results, backtesting can help build confidence in your trading approach. By seeing how your strategy would have fared in various market conditions, you can better understand its capabilities and limitations, allowing you to trade with more conviction.

The Backtesting Blueprint: The High-Level Steps for Backtesting Options Strategies

In this section, I will provide a high-level overview of the steps I take when backtesting potential trading strategies. You can use these steps as a guide for taking action when you begin to backtest your strategies.

The Backtesting Blueprint

Step 1: Define Your Strategy and Objectives

Before you begin backtesting, clearly define the options strategy you want to test, such as a butterfly, credit spread, iron condor, or any other strategy you’re interested in evaluating. Determine your backtesting objectives, such as assessing the strategy’s performance during specific market conditions or over a particular time frame.

Step 2: Select Your Backtesting Platform and Data

Choose a reliable backtesting platform that supports options trading and provides access to historical data. Ensure that the platform offers the necessary tools and features to accommodate your backtesting requirements.

When selecting historical data, consider factors such as quality, granularity, and time period covered, as these can significantly impact the accuracy and relevance of your backtesting results.

Step 3: Establish Backtesting Rules and Parameters

To maintain consistency and objectivity in your backtesting process, establish a clear set of rules and parameters that will guide your analysis. This may include:

  • Entry and exit criteria
  • Position sizing and risk management rules
  • Profit targets and stop-loss levels
  • Delta adjustment thresholds
  • Maximum trade duration

Step 4: Backtest Across Different Market Conditions

To ensure the robustness and adaptability of your options strategy, it’s essential to backtest it across various market conditions. Consider testing your strategy against historical data representing the following market scenarios:

  1. Rising Bull Market: Characterized by an upward-trending market with relatively low volatility.
  2. Sideways Market: Characterized by a range-bound market with low volatility and occasional minor spikes.
  3. Grind-Down Market: Characterized by a falling bear market with moderate volatility.
  4. Crash Market: Characterized by a rapid bear market with significant volatility spikes.

By evaluating your strategy’s performance during these different market conditions, you can assess its versatility and identify potential weaknesses that may need to be addressed.

Step 5: Set Up and Execute the Backtest

Using your chosen backtesting platform, set up your options strategy according to your established rules and parameters. Ensure that the trade details, such as contract quantities, strike prices, and expiration dates, are accurate. Once you’re confident in your setup, execute the backtest over the desired time period and market conditions.

Step 6: Analyze the Backtest

As the backtest progresses, closely monitor the strategy’s performance, paying attention to key metrics such as:

  • Profit and loss (P&L)
  • Win rate
  • Average trade duration
  • Maximum drawdown
  • Margin Requirement

Furthermore, evaluate the strategy’s behavior during different market conditions and volatility levels to gain insights into its strengths and weaknesses. Identify any patterns or anomalies that may require further investigation or adjustments to your strategy.

Step 7: Refine and Optimize Your Strategy

Based on the insights gained from your backtesting analysis, consider making data-driven refinements to your options strategy. This may involve adjusting entry and exit criteria, modifying position sizing rules, or incorporating additional risk management techniques. Be cautious not to over-optimize your strategy based on limited data, as this may lead to curve-fitting and poor real-world performance.

Step 8: Refine and Optimize Your Strategy

After refining your options strategy based on backtesting results, it’s essential to validate its performance through forward testing. Forward testing involves applying your strategy to real capital . This allows you to assess its performance under real-time market conditions and identify any potential issues or discrepancies that may not have been apparent during backtesting. Furthermore, forward testing helpt to understand trade execution or trade execution limitations and slippage.

Best Practices for Backtesting Options Strategies

To ensure the effectiveness and reliability of your backtesting process, consider the following best practices:

  • Use high-quality, granular historical data that accurately reflects the market conditions you want to test.
  • Clearly define and document your backtesting rules and parameters to maintain consistency and transparency.
  • Backtest your strategy over a sufficiently long time period to capture a diverse range of market conditions.
  • Avoid over-optimizing your strategy based on limited data, as this may lead to poor real-world performance.
  • Regularly update and refine your backtesting process as market conditions evolve and new data becomes available.

Remember, backtesting is an iterative process that requires continuous refinement and adaptation to changing market conditions. By staying committed to the process and maintaining a data-driven approach, you can develop robust and profitable options strategies that withstand the test of time.

Conclusion

Backtesting is an essential tool for options traders to evaluate and improve their strategies. By following this guide and utilizing a reliable backtesting platform, you will have a perfect starting point for effectively backtesting your options strategies, identifying areas for improvement, and, ultimately, enhancing your trading performance.

Key Takeaways

  1. Backtesting is a critical technique for options traders to evaluate their strategies using historical data, helping to model potential future performance and assess risk profiles. It can help build confidence in a trading approach by demonstrating how a strategy would have performed in various market conditions.
  2. Backtesting is akin to “paper trading” in that it provides a risk-free environment to practice and refine trading skills, allowing traders to test multiple strategy variations and learn from mistakes without risking real capital.
  3. The process of backtesting involves defining the strategy and objectives, selecting the backtesting platform and data, establishing rules and parameters, backtesting across different market conditions, analyzing results, refining the strategy, and validating it through forward testing.
  4. It’s essential to backtest strategies across various market conditions, including rising bull markets, sideways markets, grind-down markets, and crash markets, to assess versatility and identify potential weaknesses.
  5. Best practices for backtesting include using high-quality historical data, clearly defining rules and parameters, backtesting over a sufficiently long time period, avoiding over-optimization, and regularly updating and refining the backtesting process.
