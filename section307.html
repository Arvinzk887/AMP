The #1 Way to Determine If An Educator is Legitmate

After devoting hundreds of hours to vetting other trading educators, I’ve determined:

The only way to prove if an expert is credible and, more importantly profitable, is to have them share their profit and loss.

Simply put, if a trading educator doesn’t show their real profit and loss from a REPUTABLE brokerage account (ideally accompanied with a screen recording of their transaction history along with underlying trades), then why should you trust them?

Again, I believe that if an instructor makes promises in their marketing, they should prove their credibility by sharing the real P&L of their entire portfolio on a consistent basis. Also, it’s helpful to see a profit and loss per underlying trade.

So, why don’t educators share their P&L if, by doing so, they could immediately prove their credibility?

  • They Aren’t Profitable - Most trading educators simply do not have a verifiable track record of profitability since inception.The instructor or trader will simply “cherry pick” their winners and hide the losers - never consistently sharing their verifiable portfolio P&L. If they do share their P&L, it might only be a spreadsheet that is easy to fudge, or they might not share their full account statement since inception.
  • It’s Easier to Sell Shovels in a Gold Rush - Selling online courses can be likened to the Gold Rush where selling tools (shovels) was often more profitable than searching for gold itself. I’ll be the first to admit that selling educational courses is a lucrative business model provided the promises are upheld. The unfortunate reality is that more educators are more skilled in marketing - not in trading - therefore, their promises are often not upheld.
  • Common Sense Often Takes a Backseat When Greed Enters the Picture - Emotions can cloud judgment when the allure of quick profits is dangled before aspiring traders. Educators exploit this by focusing on marketing strategies that play into these emotions. This leads to potential students ignoring the red flags, such as a lack of transparency in profit and loss statements.

The broader implication here is pretty sobering: transparency simply isn’t compatible with the business model of most trading educators, yet it should be of paramount concern for the student.

Again, I implore you to do your own research. Reach out to other educators and ask for their P&L. You’ll be stunned by the number of educators who flat out won’t share their statements or will share only a snapshot of their accounts. Don’t let their seemingly legitimate excuses fool you.

So... How Can You Find The Real P&L of My Strategies?

By now I hope you understand the importance of verifying the performance of any trading strategy or educator you follow. That’s why, since 2019, I have been publicly sharing the net liquidation value and “REAL P&L” of my own trading accounts on my YouTube channel.

By posting regular updates, including weekly performance reports, trade analyses, and educational content, I aim to provide a clear, unfiltered view of how the All-Market Trading Solution performs in real- time, across various market conditions.

While past performance is never a guarantee of future results, I hope that this level of transparency allows you, as a reader and potential student, to assess the effectiveness and reliability of my strategies yourself.
