Chapter 7: The 5 Components of an All-Market Profits Trading Framework

At this point in the book, you have gained a solid understanding of the principles and concepts that form the foundation for achieving high-risk-adjusted returns regardless of market conditions.

By now, you should recognize that the key to market outperformance lies in harnessing an extractable edge while minimizing drawdowns across various market environments.

I believe this can be achieved by placing a hyper-efficient, proactively hedged, and properly positioned premium-selling option structure within a low-maintenance, mechanical, and objective trading plan. The result is a non-directional trading system that doesn’t rely on predicting future price movements while exploiting the higher-order Greeks for performance in every market condition.

However, understanding the theory is just the beginning. It is now crucial to put the puzzle pieces together and help you form a truly well-rounded “All-Market” trading strategy.

The 5 Components of the All-Market Profits Trading Framework

In the upcoming chapters, I will introduce the five core components that make up the All-Market Profits Trading Framework.

Number 1 The Trade Structure

The proactively hedged broken wing butterfly is the cornerstone of a truly non-directional strategy, but constructing this options structure requires a thorough understanding of strike selection, expiration cycles, and risk management. We will examine the specifics of how to build this trade, ensuring that you have the knowledge to create a robust, all-market position.

Number 2 Leverage and Margin

Properly utilizing leverage and margin is crucial for maximizing the potential of a trading strategy while minimizing risk. We will explore the different types of margins, such as Portfolio Margin and SPAN Margin, and discuss how to use them effectively to enhance your trading performance.

Number 3 Position Sizing

Proper position sizing is critical for managing risk and pre- serving capital. We will delve into the art and science of determining optimal capital allocation, taking into account factors such as account size, risk tolerance, and market conditions.

Number 4 Entry Rules

Knowing when and how to enter your trades is as important as constructing the right structure. In this section, I will provide you with “trade execution” principles to manage risk.

Number 5 Management Rules

Once you’ve entered a trade, the real work begins. In this section, I will equip you with guidelines, triggers, and approaches for managing trades.

Each of these components is essential to ensuring the strategy’s success, and we will explore them in-depth, providing you with the foundation, principles, and guidelines needed to implement a truly non-directional strategy.

The Road Ahead

In the coming chapters, we will cover each component in detail, providing examples, case studies, and practical guidelines. The goal is not just to learn about the All-Market Profits Trading Framework but to gain a deep understanding of its principles and guidelines. By the end of this section, you will have a solid foundation to build upon and adapt an “All-Market Profits” type strategy to your own trading style and risk tolerance.
