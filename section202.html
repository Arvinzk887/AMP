 Rising From the Ashes 

Blowing up your account and going into debt will turn you into a different person. I knew that if I was to ever have success with trading, I needed to arm myself with a different set of tools, beliefs, and, most importantly, a set of clear, well-defined goals.

Itâ€™s essential to define what success looks like to me. Trading is only worth my time and effort if it meets three key criteria:

  1. It needs to outperform the S&P on a risk-adjusted basis (otherwise, I would simply invest in an index fund).
  2. It needs to be passive. Trading should be a means to an end, not an end in itself. I only really want to spend about 20 minutes a week trading (I want to trade for a living and actually live).
  3. Finally, it needs to be safe, objective, and stress-free. I want to be able to go on vacation and not necessarily have to manage my account or look at my computer all the time.

 Ultimatel, my goal is to seek peaceful passive trading profits:

Optimize For Peaceful Trading Profits

What that means is I optimize for high risk-adjusted returns, market outperformance, and making money in all market types. To achieve this goal, I obviously need high returns, and therefore, I need to harness a true trading edge.

Next, I need to mitigate and control risk at all costs. This means low drawdowns and being safe in crashes so I can sleep easy at night and profit without panic. Finally, I need to eliminate the hassle. By this, I mean minimizing management complexity, emotions, and mental bandwidth. I use objective trading rules and limit my trading to around 20 minutes a week. 

In other words, I needed to treat trading like a business. This is when I developed my options trading laboratory.
