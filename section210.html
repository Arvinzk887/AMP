The Grind-Down Market

Definition: A Grind-Down Market is a sustained period where the market prices decline by a certain percentage (around 10 percent plus) over a given timeframe.

Example: A Grind-Down Market occurred from October 1, 2018, through December 31, 2018, when the market was down 14 point 5 percent with a maximum VIX spike of approximately 25 points.

A.M.P. Strategy Performance: From September 20, 2018 to December 24, 2018, the market (S&P 500) drew down by negative 19 poin seven three percent while the A.M.P. increased by 11 point five seven percent . From January 3, 2022 to October 12, 2022, the market (S&P 500) drew down by negative 25 point four three percent while the A.M.P. increased by 18 point six nine percent.
